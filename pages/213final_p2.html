e-publishing

information-seeking behavior

The development of e-publishing has changed the way libraries collect materials. With more libraries relying upon digital resources, patrons are relying less on browsing the stacks and are instead spending more research time honing search terms to locate a narrower scope of resources. Libraries and the digital platforms they host are thus now tasked with fine tuning information-seeking behavior to better serve patrons whose research no longer accommodates a serendipitous approach to locating information. Articles located using digital discovery tools meanwhile may be considered in a vacuum, patrons ignoring the editorial work that ties together specific journal issues and volumes under a broad theme.

While some researchers may still prefer to browse the stacks, or follow specific publications and know to look for related materials within them, new methods for locating resources in a digital space leave less experienced researchers out. The resources included below for further reading explore information-seeking behavior in the digital realm. If academic libraries are to continue as integral parts of research institutions, they must find new ways of guiding patrons to find the best resources, which might not be found without a bit of serendipity.
