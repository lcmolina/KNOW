e-publishing


peer review & labor

For many working in the profession of librarianship there seems to often be the notion that technological advances will threaten traditional roles. But e-publishing requires expertise from library workers who know and understand the needs for the communities and institutions which they serve. It is the responsibility of those who work in the library to seek to deepen their relationships with new platforms for publishing and learning; and doing so can bring validation to librarianship for those who may have felt its relevance at risk.

Peer to peer review of work submitted for publishing is an act of respect and patience which involves labor often undermined by e-pub services which take advantage of the idea but don’t enforce its (much needed) action. It is not without notice that journals with quality contributors may move towards open access in hopes to continue with peer to peer review on their own terms.
