e-publishing

policies and politics

The judgements on value made when deciding to invest in e-publishing platforms reflect specific policies and politics of the institutions behind them. Library collection development policies, as written, may reflect an interest in providing access to resources through emerging technologies, or, they may need to be updated or changed to allow for a shift away from a focus on tangible material collections. An evaluation of cost is often at the forefront of decisions to go digital as libraries consider e-published resources. There are a variety of considerations that go into maintaining or updating the policies of institutions subscribing to e-publishing platforms, some of which are reflected in the below perspectives. Many others are yet to be articulated and may come in the form of opinions from the inside.
