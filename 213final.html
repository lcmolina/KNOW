intro

to whom it may concern...

The move toward digital publishing provides both opportunities and roadblocks to accessing information. As a relatively new issue in library acquisitions, many professionals are still ambivalent on the matter. While some professionals and patrons remain skeptical about the use of electronic media as a replacement for tangible resources and exchanges, the ease of accessing online materials nevertheless results in these tools becoming heavily relied upon for research across a broad range of users. With corporations like Elsevier starting to take on large portions of available resources it remains to be seen if libraries will be able to keep up with or provide alternatives to the high cost demanded by such platforms.

This project is intended as an access point for exploring the many issues--opportunities and challenges alike--facing the information profession as publishing turns increasingly digital. KNOWe-publishing’s creators are not experts in the field of e-publishing, but through their own curiosity and efforts to become better informed have compiled the resources found in this guide. The project began with the intention to write a series of short perspective pieces, inspired by the Radical Teacher forum related to that publication’s own shift to a digital format. Realizing the vast extent and interrelated issues e-publishing presents, and the impossibility of providing strong opinions for or against e-publishing as a general platform, the creators opted instead for a comprehensive educational research guide, a starting point for becoming better informed new information professionals dealing with e-publishing resources.

Preliminary themes for KNOWe-publishing’s initial presentation have been identified as: information-seeking behavior, peer review and labor, aspirational diversity, policies and politics, open access, and systems and infrastructures. These general topics may morph over time as ideas about e-publishing are honed and developed. Tags used on this site are intended to demonstrate the interrelatedness of ideas among the identified themes.

This guide is intended as a participatory platform, and will offer the opportunity to publish perspectives, opinions, and additional resources to make this site a comprehensive compendium on the topic of e-publishing. It is hoped that through providing a central hub for ideas about e-publishing, this broad and complicated issue can be better understood and utilized by the individuals and institutions who increasingly rely upon access to the resources e-publishing provides.
